# Sero — a 15-minute test drive 🙏

Thanks for trying this. Sero helps you **prepare a 1:1** with someone on your team: you tell it a little about the person and what's on your mind, and it gives you a focused plan for the conversation. It's an early version — we want your honest first impression.

---

## Before you start
- Have a **real 1:1 coming up**, with a **real person on your team**, in mind.
- Use a **laptop or desktop** (it's a "prep at your desk" tool).
- About **15 minutes**, one sitting.

## Do this
1. Go to **sero.team** → click **Create an account** (use your real work email).
2. Answer the short **setup questions** — who the 1:1 is with, and what's on your mind. First name only is fine.
3. Read the **prep brief** it gives you — *"what to walk in with."* This is the bit we most want your take on.
4. It'll then ask you a few questions and end with a **briefing**. Go as far as feels useful.

## Then tell us — just reply with 3 quick answers
1. Did the prep brief give you **something useful** for that meeting? *(yes / sort of / no — one line on why)*
2. Would you **use this before your next 1:1**? *(yes / no)*
3. Where did you **get stuck or confused**?

## Please don't
- Don't use **made-up data** — the value only shows with a real person and a real situation.
- Don't enter anything **sensitive** you wouldn't want stored — a first name or initials is plenty.
- Don't **overthink** your answers. Quick and honest beats polished.

---

## Optional — see what your teammate would see
If you'd like to see the other side: on the **Team** page, click **Tidy up → Invite**, enter a teammate's email, and send them the link it gives you. When they join, they see **only the date and type** of the 1:1s you prepped about them — **never your notes**. (Skip this if you don't have someone handy — it's not essential.)
