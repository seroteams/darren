# Phase 3 — Privacy note + first run

**Part of:** [PLAN.md](PLAN.md) · **Track:** A (ship-blocker) · **Status:** ⬜

## Goal
A brand-new manager understands what Sero does with their data and can get to their first 1:1 without
being told how.

## Changes
- A plain-language privacy/consent note: what we store, who can see it, how to delete it — shown at signup
  and linked in-app.
- First-run experience: sensible empty states (no blank screens), and a clear "run your first 1:1" path
  that walks a new manager from nothing to a finished briefing.

## Not in this phase
- Deep onboarding tutorials or tooltips everywhere — just the first-run path.
- Feedback capture and the one-pager (Phase 5).

## Done when
- [ ] A fresh account is guided to its first completed 1:1 without outside help.
- [ ] Empty states read as intentional, not broken.
- [ ] The privacy note is readable and reachable.
- [ ] Product owner has tested the scenarios below and said go.

## Test scenarios — for the product owner
Walk through these yourself. Next phase waits for your green light.
1. **Cold start** — Register a brand-new account. Without me guiding you, follow the screen to run your first 1:1 to a finished briefing. You should never hit a dead end or a blank page. ❌ Not OK if you get stuck or need me.
2. **Privacy in plain words** — Find and read the privacy note. You should understand what's stored and who sees it in under a minute. ❌ Not OK if it's missing, jargon-y, or hidden.
3. **No scary blanks** — Visit a section with no data yet. You should see a friendly "nothing here yet, do X" message. ❌ Not OK if it looks broken or empty-white.
