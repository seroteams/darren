# GTM validation plan — the first corridor test

**One page. Who tries Sero first, what we watch, and what "they'd use it" looks like.**

## 1. Who — the first corridor-test managers (2–3, friendly)

Pick people who are all of these:
- **Actually manage people** and run (or should run) regular 1:1s — not founders, not peers being polite.
- **Trusted enough for honesty** — they'll tell Carl "this was useless" to his face.
- **Reachable weekly** — a corridor test needs a second week, not a one-off demo.
- **Consent is clean** — they're OK putting real (first-name) notes about their people into the alpha.

## 2. How — the corridor-test mechanics

- **Don't demo. Watch.** They drive from the login screen; Carl only unblocks, never steers.
- **First session:** they prep one real upcoming 1:1 (real person, first name only). Carl watches where they hesitate, what they skip, what they read twice.
- **Then leave them alone for a week.** No reminders. The whole point is whether they come back unprompted.
- **Week-2 check-in:** 15 minutes. Did they use the briefing in the real conversation? Did they come back to prep the next one?
- **Low ratings are wanted.** A ★2 "not useful" is signal, not failure — never coach them toward kindness.

## 3. What to watch for (write these down per manager)

- Do they finish the first prep, or stall mid-flow? Where exactly?
- Do they use **real names and real situations**, or keep it hypothetical? (Hypothetical = they don't trust it yet.)
- Do they **take the briefing into the actual 1:1** (screen open, printed, or copied)?
- Do they **come back unprompted** to prep the next 1:1? How many days later?
- Do they rate their runs, and what do the ratings say?
- The magic-moment tell: do they say some version of **"can I keep using this?"** without being asked.

## 4. The bar — what "they'd use it" looks like

**Pass (per manager):** within ~2 weeks they run a **second** prep for a real 1:1, **unprompted**, and say the briefing helped the actual conversation. That's the return-visit bar the research set — anything less is politeness.

- **Strong pass:** 2 of 3 managers hit the bar → start talking about a small **paid pilot** (the research's next step).
- **Weak signal:** they praise it but don't return → the first-run wow isn't enough; revisit what would pull them back (the parked continuity loop is the known candidate — stays parked until Carl says go).
- **Fail:** stalls mid-flow or hypothetical-only use → fix the flow before widening the circle. A fail here is cheap; the same fail after hosting + onboarding isn't.

## 5. What this is NOT

No hosting decision, no pricing, no outreach beyond the 2–3 names above, no feature promises made to testers. One corridor, honest notes, then decide.
