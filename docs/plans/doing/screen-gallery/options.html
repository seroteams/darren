10 ways to choose a screen — without the mess

Same job every time: get from "I want to see screen X" to that screen, fast, then back out cleanly. These differ in where the list lives and whether anything covers the page.

What went wrong with the current one: it added a floating panel next to your app's nav rail — and your rail expands on hover, so the two collide. The dark wash was a backdrop I added to make the panel pop. Options below mostly drop both problems.
1Command bar★ top pick

Hit / (or click a search box). Type "brief", press Enter — you're there. The list only appears as you type. No panel, no dark. Fastest once you know the names.

2Top dropdown

A “Screens ▾” button in the top bar drops a menu down under it. Pick one, it closes. Familiar as a normal dropdown; nothing slides over the page.

3Fold into your nav★ tidy

No second panel at all — the screen list becomes a “Gallery” section inside your existing left nav (the one that already opens on hover). One panel, ever. Can't collide with itself.

4All-screens grid★ visual

The logo/“Screens” opens a full-page grid of screen cards (like your Tests page). Click a card → it fills the screen with a small “← All screens” to go back. Great for browsing; zero overlap.

5Push, don't cover

The list slides in and pushes the screen across instead of covering it — both fully visible, nothing dimmed. Toggle to slide it back. Needs the screen to be OK a bit narrower while open.

6Top tab strip

A thin horizontal strip of screen names across the top (scrolls sideways, grouped). Always there, never a column. Good if you hop between a handful a lot; busy with 40+.

7Prev / next stepper

‹ Prev · Screen name · Next › in the bar — step through screens one at a time, plus a small jump menu. Lovely for reviewing every screen in order; slow for jumping to one specific.

8Floating launcher

A small round floating button (corner) pops a compact list right beside it; pick and it closes. Sits on top, touches nothing else — but it's an extra floating dot on every gallery screen.

9Right-side panel

Same slide-out list, but from the right edge — nowhere near the nav rail, so no collision, and no need to dim. The list just sits away from everything else.

10Breadcrumb only
/

Ultra-minimal: just a “Gallery / About ▾” breadcrumb in the bar with a small jump menu, plus typing the name in the address bar. Almost no UI at all. Leanest, least discoverable.

My steer: if you want it to feel effortless and never cover the page, #1 Command bar (type-to-jump) or #4 All-screens grid (browse cards) are the cleanest.
