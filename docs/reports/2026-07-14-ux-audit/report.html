Sero, walked as a manager and as a member

Every screen of both public personas, live-walked at desktop and phone width, measured against Sero's own design rules and standard UX practice — then put in front of a four-agency review panel.

Sero wins the first session and loses the second. The visual identity and the briefing content are genuinely good — better than most funded products. But the return path (the exact thing the validation gate measures) is broken in three places, and the member gets the thinnest, most broken surface in the product.

Four independent agency-lens reviews scored it 4–5.5 out of 10 and converged, unprompted, on the same five problems. None of them are visual-polish problems. All of them are fixable in days, not months.

Nielsen bands: most real products score 20–32. The manager side is mid-pack with strong peaks; the member side is below the floor of what ships. Every panel score said the same thing: strong foundation, weak follow-through.

The five things all four agencies agree on

  1. The return path is broken, and it's the one thing being measured. Resume can fail into a dead-end alert and the broken row keeps offering Resume. Three surfaces answer "where do I start my next 1:1?" three different ways — above the list, below the fold, or not at all. Publicis called this "the single highest-risk defect in the product" at a 3-manager sample size.
    P0M1 M2 M3
  2. The member experience was never designed — it was disclosed. Two different home screens depending on how they arrive, an About page written for their manager, a forbidden "Start a 1:1" button that walks them two steps into the manager's private prep flow before failing silently. IDEO: "a receipt, not a relationship."
    P0B1 B2 B3 B4 B5 B7
  3. Internal QA machinery is on the customer stage. One click from Home, a paying manager sees engine hashes, "0/8 judged", Pass/Fail on "Does not over-infer", and a Keep/Fix/Block verdict. IDEO: it "retroactively poisons confidence in every briefing." All four said: remove it from the manager role now, don't polish it.
    P1M4
  4. The product doesn't know its own name for its core object. Session, run, 1:1, prep, One-page run, Live Q&A, Synthesis — and a dialog where "Cancel setup" opens "Reset session?" with a "Cancel" button that means don't-cancel. Kaiser X: "every synonym is a small withdrawal from the calm-advisor persona."
    P1M7 M13 M14 B4
  5. History doesn't compound. People created during intake never join the Team roster, so the promise "their 1:1 history fills in as you meet" silently breaks — and accumulating per-person context is the only nudge-free reason a manager returns. Publicis: "the value-accrual engine failing."
    P1M10

The agency panel

⚠️ Honesty note: no real agencies were contacted. Each review is an independent AI assessment locked to that agency's published philosophy and method, run blind to the other three. Treat them as four disciplined outside lenses, not as quotes from the firms.
Publicis Sapient5/10
Business transformation · does UX move the metric
"Sero wins the first session and loses the second — and the second session is the entire validation metric. Output-polished, outcome-fragile."
  • Resume dead-end = the #1 defect: one failed return ≈ one lost corridor manager out of three (M3, escalated).
  • M1+M2 are one job — "the return path" — not two findings.
  • QA verdict page is a positioning leak: hide it, don't polish it (M4).
  • M10 is "the value-accrual engine failing," not a data bug.
  • Downgrades most member findings until Gate 1 passes — except B2, which damages the buyer second-hand.
Missed by the audit — no instrumentation of the return metric; the built-in same-day return loop (prep → meeting → briefing) never assessed; time-per-prepped-1:1 never measured against a blank doc.
Kaiser X Labs5/10
Trust architecture · brand-led experience
"All the design care flows to the powerful one; the member receives the thinnest, coldest, most broken surface in the product. The foundation deserves a better building."
  • B3/B5/B7 are one missing thing — a designed member experience — not three patches.
  • B2 is a trust breach with reputational cost per occurrence, not a usability bug.
  • M13+M7+B4 = "the product's voice stammers": one language system defect.
  • Backstage-on-frontstage is a policy gap, wider than the one QA page (M4 + build hash + "qa-overnight Priya").
Missed by the audit — "★ 4.5 avg" next to a person's name reads as rating the human; the privacy promise is asserted but never demonstrable by the member; the briefing (the product's best moment) has no ceremony.
IDEO4/10
Human-centred design · both sides of the power imbalance
"Sero has designed half a relationship. A richer surveillance log is still a surveillance log — the member needs an input, not a better output."
  • B1–B4 are one wound: nobody designed the member's arrival.
  • M1/M2 elevated: the "minute before walking in" is the product's peak moment and it's undesigned.
  • M4's real cost: seeing "No private leak" being checked poisons trust in every briefing.
  • "Prepped about you" is "the most damaging six characters in the product."
Missed by the audit — the member is never asked anything by the product; the privacy promise is emotionally double-edged ("your manager writes things you may never see"); the ritual has no ending — nothing marks what was agreed, for either person.
Frog5.5/10
Craft · design-system integrity
"Sero has written itself a good design system and then declined to build it. Currently a set of pages that share a font, not a system — fixable in weeks, not quarters."
  • M1/M2/B4/M15 = one missing component: a canonical run-list with a mandated action slot.
  • B1/B2/B3/B6/M9 = one architectural gap: identity/role resolved per-app instead of once.
  • M7/M13/M14/B5 = untokenized language; the dialog is the vocabulary failure made dangerous.
  • M4 is deliberate per the router comment — "a governance failure, not an oversight."
Missed by the audit — the seams between states are unstyled (bounces, native alerts — "where calm dies"); the system has no enforcement mechanism; empty states are carrying load-bearing functionality (the only Start on Past 1:1s vanishes when the screen succeeds).

Fix next — ranked, validation-first

  1. One canonical return path. Primary "Prep your next 1:1" above every list (person page, Past 1:1s, Home already right); Resume self-heals instead of dead-ending. Smallest change with a direct line to the unprompted-return metric.
    fixes M1 M2 M3~1 day
  2. Close the member gates. One role-resolved home per app (login and boot agree); role-gate /new and every start CTA; retire the dead member run-detail route.
    fixes B1 B2 B6 M9~1 day
  3. Move the QA verdict page behind the internal flag. Near-zero effort, immediate trust protection at the willingness-to-pay moment.
    fixes M4~1 hour
  4. One language sweep. One noun ("1:1"), human stage names, the dialog grammar fix, the member-voice rewrites, and the About page in the member's voice. All copy, no logic.
    fixes M7 M13 M14 B3 B4 B5~1 day
  5. Make history compound. Intake-created people auto-join Team; the Team card meta stops contradicting itself.
    fixes M10~½ day
  6. Craft batch (after the above). Clickable person cards (M8), person deep-links (M9 remainder), invite link into the sheet (M11), top-bar overflow + single progress counter (M5), one-accent expanded state (M6), minimal account settings (M12), mobile row wrapping (M15).
    P2 batch~2 days
