Getting ready to share latest

Sero is becoming a real product you sign into — with a home of your own, the people you meet with, and every past 1:1 kept safe. And it fits in your pocket now.

The pre-go-live "manager-ready" track — turning the internal tool into something a real manager can be handed. Foundation phases are done; this is the make-it-shippable pass. Most items below are built and awaiting Carl's QA walk.

  • 🔁The Q&A won't ask twice. Once you've explained what's behind a snag, Sero won't circle back a few questions later and ask the same thing in different words — it moves on to fresh ground. A genuinely new angle on it (like what would take the pressure off) can still come up; the same what-and-why won't.
  • 🧘The 1:1 itself got calmer. The whole app now follows one design language: your progress bar stays in view right through the conversation, the answer box behaves like any notes field (Enter makes a new line), your recap appears the moment it's ready instead of drip-feeding, and the prep brief has one clean, consistent look. Less to think about, more room for the conversation.
  • 🎨design-consolidation P0-P4 (✅ LIVE 2026-07-23/24, PRs #29 + #30, $0 — free checks + Carl's walks). The 45-screen design audit became the acceptance criteria; five phases live: shared kit + manager lists on one table/toolbar, one branded auth shell, recomposed member Home, flow spine (stepper Setup→Recap, one wizard footer, one interstitial), interview calm-down (stepper visible, ≤3 actions, Esc-skip gone, Enter = newline), instant briefing render, and ONE customer Prepare layout (Sheet via DEFAULT_VARIANT; the 12-variant lab fenced behind isInternalAdmin, closing a manager-reachable leak). Still open: P5 labelled sidebar, P6 admin sweep, P7 re-audit; SeroEngine rename parked.
  • 🚪Done early? Wrap up properly. From question 4 onward, the old "Skip to briefing" escape becomes "Wrap up — get my briefing": one warm confirm, one real closing question, then your briefing — so a 1:1 you end early still gets a proper goodbye and a chance to agree next actions, not a jump-cut. Before question 4 nothing changes.
  • 🤝Your 1:1s keep their promises now. End a 1:1 by locking in what you both agreed — and your next 1:1 with that person opens by checking those promises off, one tap each (done / partly / not yet / things changed). Yours are listed first. In a rush? Skip, and they simply come back next time. Nothing is ever marked done unless you say so.
  • 🤝Agreeing next steps is now its own moment. When the questions finish, you land on one clear page before your recap: what you promise and what they promise, side by side. Fix the wording, move a promise to whoever really owns it, lock it in — and your recap (and the PDF you can save) shows exactly who agreed to what. Trying Sero as a guest? You get the same page, and your PDF keeps the real agreement too.

Snapshot + both apps' rehydrate carry promises; a resetSession leak (flags missing from initial) found + regression-tested. 157/157 (+8), typecheck clean.

  • ✉️Inviting your team is one clean flow now. A new Members page shows everyone who can log in — name, email, role, status — with one "Invite people" button. Adding someone to your team is just a name (no email needed), and when you do want them on Sero, one invite does it: change roles, deactivate, resend or revoke, all from the same page. The old confusing "link an existing account" dropdown is gone.
  • 👀Your Team page tells you where each invite stands. Every person now carries one clear status — Not on Sero → Invited → Opened the link → On Sero — so you can see who's stuck where, and nudge them with a one-tap Remind. The invite popup now says plainly what they'll see when they log in.
  • 🔒Quiet security hardening. Standard browser security protections now ride on every page, and internal AI keys travel more safely behind the scenes. Nothing changes on screen — it's just safer.
  • 📋The end of your 1:1 is now your Recap — and it reads like one. What you get after the conversation used to be called a "Briefing" — the same word as the prep you get before it. It's now your Recap, and it's been rebuilt to read as a story: what came out, the honest read, then what to do next — with what you two agreed lifted into its own block at the end, where you'll actually act on it. Your private reflection is clearly marked as yours alone, separate from the part that's fine to share. And when a conversation was too thin to read much, Sero says so plainly at the top instead of burying it.
  • 🎯Fresh topics each time. When you prep another 1:1 with the same person, Sero steers toward ground you haven't covered yet — so your third check-in doesn't re-run the same list. Mention an old topic in your notes and it comes straight back; a real concern always wins.
  • Your prep remembers last time. Prepping your next 1:1 with someone now fills in what you agreed last time and what to watch for — ready to edit or clear before you start.
  • Your team is real now. Start a 1:1 by picking the person — no retyping. Rename or merge duplicates and it sticks everywhere. And a team member you link can sign in and see when their 1:1s happened — just the when and with whom, never your notes.
  • Works on your phone. The whole 1:1 flow, from setup to briefing, on mobile.
  • Sign in to your own space. A home page, your team, and your past 1:1s — just yours.
  • A Team that builds itself. The people you meet with appear automatically from the 1:1s you run.
  • Rate and revisit. Give each conversation a 1–5 rating and reopen any past briefing.
  • A cleaner, consistent look across every screen.
  • Try it before signing up — and keep it. The front page starts a guest 1:1 in one click, no account needed. Finish it and Sero offers to save it: create a free account and that 1:1 becomes the first entry in your own history. What you type stays private to you.
  • 🔑Forgot your password? You can reset it now. A "Forgot password?" link on the login screen emails you a secure, one-time link to set a new one — good for an hour, then it stops working. Works the same for managers, members and admins. For your safety, the "we've sent you a link" message looks identical whether or not the email has an account.
  • 🗄️Nothing gets lost — ever. Your whole 1:1 history now lives in a real cloud database (your full back-catalogue included), so it's there on any device and survives anything that happens to one machine.
  • 🎯Even more honest. Four new built-in checks (18 now) stop the AI inventing feelings nobody stated, skipping evidence, or glossing over thin notes.
  • 🗑️Your words, your call. On "Words of each role", hover any AI-suggested word and delete it — it disappears from the page and stops being used in your 1:1s. Changed your mind? "Hidden words" puts it back with one click; the AI's original is never lost.
  • 👓Easier on the eyes. Reading text lifted across the app — less squinting, same screens.
  • 📦Your app is now its own app. The app you use was rebuilt as a fully separate product from our internal workshop — none of our testing or admin tooling is ever downloaded to your browser, and an automatic check proves it on every change. Same screens, cleaner and lighter under the hood.

Your first time through, a short guide lays out the three steps — who it's with, your notes, your briefing — with a real example of what useful notes look like, and it steps aside once you've run one. Plus a wording pass so every screen calls the same thing by the same name.

The core

The heart of Sero: prepare a great 1:1, run it live, and get an honest wrap-up — in your team's own language.

The engine and the manager-facing run that everything else is built around.

  • One-page run. The whole conversation on a single page that grows as you go.
  • Your team's words. Teach Sero the vocabulary your roles actually use (job lexicons).
  • Five kinds of 1:1 — bi-weekly, performance, growth, "something feels off," and onboarding — each with its own natural shape.
  • Live scoring on four things — wellbeing, engagement, clarity, growth — earned only from what's actually said.
  • The pipeline: focus areas → prep brief → question bank → live per-turn planner → final briefing (+ optional phrase harvest).
  • Content: a 4,400+-question library, 31 cached role profiles, neutral-seed axes, dedup + question-integrity gates.
  • QA cockpit: run library, side-by-side Compare, 8-dimension verdicts, and an offline regression check.
