# SERO — Roadmap

**The strategic arc: from a single-user engine to an environment managers and teams can use.**
Driver: Carl. Created 2026-06-15.

This is the **direction** doc, not the to-do. For what's in flight this week, see
[SERO_BOARD.md](../SERO_BOARD.md). The board's **Next** column is fed from the current milestone
below. Rule of thumb: strategy and milestones live here; task status lives on the board.

## North star
*(one line — to confirm in the interview)*
A manager can prepare for, run, and follow up on a real 1:1 — and trust every word — without it
ever becoming surveillance.

## Who it's for
*(to fill from the interview: buyer / user / beneficiary / ICP)*

## Non-goals — what SERO will not become
Carried from the board's Cut/Parked decisions; confirm + extend in the interview.
- No executive dashboards, sentiment trends, or HR analytics.
- No surveillance of employees.
- Not a generic coaching content library.
- Historical data = session continuity only, never trend-mining.

## Milestones (hypothesis — to be ordered & sharpened in the interview)

> Each milestone gets an **Outcome** (what's true when it's done) and a **Done means** checklist
> once the interview settles the order. Status: `planned` until then.

### M0 — Hardened single-user engine  · status: in progress
The existing app, made unbreakable for one manager: session resume, deterministic fallbacks,
better intake, cleaner flow. (This is the existing [next-stage](todo/next-stage/PLAN.md) build.)

### M1 — Pilot-ready  · status: planned
A handful of real managers can use it, hand-held, and come back. What's the minimum to put it in
a friendly manager's hands?

### M2 — Accounts & data  · status: planned
Logins, real data storage, privacy posture. The single→multi-user plumbing.

### M3 — Team layer  · status: planned
A role above the individual manager — and the visibility/trust rules that go with it.

### M4 — Scale  · status: planned
Reliability, cost, integrations, onboarding at volume.

---

## Open decisions (resolving via the 2026-06-15 interview)
These three forks gate everything downstream:
1. **Finish line** — what is the first real use, and when?
2. **Single→multi** — when do real accounts get built (vs. hand-held pilots first)?
3. **Team layer & visibility** — does anyone sit above the manager, and what can they see?
